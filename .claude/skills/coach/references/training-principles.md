# Training principles

The "why" behind the rules. Read only when the user challenges a decision and you need to explain it.

## 80/20 polarized training

**80% of weekly running time at easy/aerobic effort. 20% at high intensity.** Empirical finding from elite endurance athletes (Seiler et al.). Most amateurs run their easy too hard and their hard not hard enough — and stagnate.

**Implication:** when in doubt, swap intensity for easy, not the reverse.

## Supercompensation & overload

Training stress + recovery → fitness gain (CTL rises). Training stress without recovery → injury / staleness (CTL stalls or falls, ATL stays high).

**Implication:** TSB cycles matter. Productive TSB range is -10 to -20 most of the time, dipping to +5 to +15 before a race (taper) and back to -15 after a recovery week.

## Aerobic base predicts everything

For events lasting >5 minutes, aerobic capacity (CTL, mitochondrial density, capillary network) sets the ceiling. You can't sprint-train your way to a 10K PR.

**Implication:** never trade volume for intensity in the base phase. The 10K block (weeks 9-12) only works because of the aerobic platform built in weeks 1-8.

## Specificity in the final 4-6 weeks

Last 4-6 weeks before a goal race must include race-pace, race-format work. Otherwise the engine is built but the gear isn't installed.

**Implication for Thomas:**
- Weeks 9-12 = 10K block, race-pace work (1km reps @ 4:00/km)
- Weeks 13-20 = lap-event specific (520m cold-start reps with walk recovery)

## Hard days hard, easy days easy

The single biggest amateur mistake: gray-zone running. Quality sessions need to be at quality intensity. Easy sessions need to be EASY (slower than feels comfortable, even).

**Implication:** if a quality session was clearly slower than target, the cause is usually inadequate recovery on easy days — not lack of effort on quality day. Look at the easy-day pace before adjusting the quality session.

## Recovery is training

Adaptation happens during rest. Skipping recovery is not "tougher" — it's blocking the gains you trained for.

**Implication:** rest days are non-negotiable. Don't replace them with "easy" runs unless legs feel exceptional.

## Long run is irreplaceable

Long runs build:
- Mitochondrial density
- Glycogen storage
- Capillary network in slow-twitch fibers
- Connective tissue resilience
- Mental durability

No other session does all of this. Two short runs ≠ one long run.

**Implication:** never skip the long run unless injury or illness. Move it within the week if needed, but get it in.

## Strength for injury prevention (Thomas-specific)

Shin/knee history → strength work isn't optional. Calf, glute, and tibialis strength prevent the two failure modes:
- Tibial stress: weak tibialis posterior + calf
- Patellofemoral pain: weak glutes / hip stability

**Implication:** never replace strength sessions with running. If short on time, drop a run and keep strength.

## Tapering science

For events ≥10K, the standard taper:
- 14 days out: drop volume 20%
- 7 days out: drop volume 50%, maintain intensity (sharpness)
- Race week: 70-80% volume drop, 1-2 short tune-ups, full rest day before

**Implication:** week 19 (taper) and week 20 (race) are templates with very specific structure. Don't add volume to feel productive — that loses the taper benefit.

## Cold-start specificity (24h lap event)

The lap event isn't a continuous race — it's 10 separate cold sprints over 24h with hours of rest. Training for a continuous race wastes preparation.

**The specific stimulus:**
- Wake from rest (or near-rest) state
- Brief (<10 min) warmup
- All-out 90-second effort
- Recovery, repeat hours later

**Training mirror:** Cold-start protocol weeks 13-20. 5 min dynamic warmup only, no jog, then 520m at 1:18-1:20, walk recovery 5-8 min, repeat. Trains the EXACT energy system + neural pattern.

## When to break rules

Rules above are defaults. Override when:

- Athlete reports injury → defaults yield to medical caution
- Athlete reports overload despite "good" data → trust the athlete, not the curves
- 6 months into the same plan with no progress → the plan needs change, not the athlete
- Race date moves → re-seed, don't patch

If overriding a rule, log to coach_log with `--reason "rule override: <which>, <why>"`.
